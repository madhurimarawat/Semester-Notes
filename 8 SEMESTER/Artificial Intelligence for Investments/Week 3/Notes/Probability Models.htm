Probability Models 🎲

Feature 📜 Student's t-distribution 📊 Poisson Distribution 📈 Chi-Square Distribution 🎯 Binomial Distribution 🎲 Exponential Distribution ⏳
Definition 📖 Used when population variance is unknown Models the probability of events occurring in a fixed interval Used in tests of independence and goodness of fit Models the number of successes in a fixed number of trials Models time until the next event in a Poisson process
Formula 📏 \( t = \frac{X - \mu}{s / \sqrt{n}} \) \( P(X = k) = \frac{e^{-\lambda} \lambda^k}{k!} \) \( \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O - E)^2}{E} \) \( P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} \) \( f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x} \)
Key Properties 🔍 Symmetric, heavier tails than normal distribution Discrete, skewed right, mean equals variance Positively skewed, sum of squared normal variables Discrete, two possible outcomes (success/failure) Continuous, memoryless property
Use Cases 🎯 Used in hypothesis testing, confidence intervals Used in queuing theory, network traffic analysis Used in categorical data analysis Used in quality control, election predictions Used in reliability engineering, survival analysis
Real-life Example 🌍 Estimating the mean height of students in a school Counting the number of calls received in an hour Testing if a die is fair Determining the probability of getting heads 5 times in 10 coin tosses Predicting time between failures of a machine
