Sampling Methods 🎯

Method 📜 Definition 📖 Methodology 🛠️ Formula 📏 Use Cases 🎯 Real-life Example 🌍 Advantages ✅
Simple Random Sampling 🎲 Every individual has an equal chance of selection Randomly selecting samples using a lottery or random number generator \( P = \frac{1}{N} \) Used in surveys, elections, and experiments Selecting employees randomly for a company survey Eliminates bias, easy to implement
Stratified Random Sampling 📊 Population is divided into subgroups (strata) before sampling Randomly selecting samples from each subgroup \( n_i = \frac{N_i}{N} \times n \) Used in studies where population has distinct categories Dividing students into grade levels before selecting samples Ensures representation, reduces variability
Cluster Sampling 🏘️ Population is divided into clusters, some clusters are randomly selected Randomly selecting entire groups instead of individuals \( k = \frac{N}{n} \) Used in large-scale surveys and geographic studies Choosing cities randomly for a national survey Cost-effective, easier for large populations
Systematic Sampling 🔄 Every nth individual is selected from a list Choosing a starting point and selecting every nth item \( k = \frac{N}{n} \) Used in quality control and manufacturing Inspecting every 10th item on a production line Simple to use, ensures even coverage
