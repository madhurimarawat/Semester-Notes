Data Warehouse Schema Models 📊

Properties 📜 Star Schema ⭐ Snowflake Schema ❄️ Fact Constellation Schema 🌌
Definition 📖 Denormalized structure with a central fact table linked to dimension tables. Normalized version of the star schema where dimension tables are further divided. Complex schema with multiple fact tables sharing dimension tables.
Structure 🏗️ Simple, with direct relationships between fact and dimension tables. More complex, with multiple levels of dimension tables. Most complex, containing multiple fact tables with shared dimensions.
Normalization Level 🔄 Low (denormalized, minimal joins). High (normalized, reducing redundancy). Medium to high (fact tables normalized, dimensions may be shared).
Complexity ⚙️ Simple Intermediate High
Query Performance 🚀 Fast, as fewer joins are required. Slower due to multiple joins. Slower compared to star and snowflake schemas.
Storage Requirements 💾 Higher due to redundancy. Lower, as normalization reduces data duplication. Medium to high, as multiple fact tables are involved.
Real-Life Examples 🌟 Retail sales reporting, e-commerce dashboards. Banking transaction systems, ERP systems. Healthcare analytics, telecommunication data models.
Use Cases 🎯 Best for simple and fast query performance. Best for reducing storage space and maintaining data integrity. Best for complex analytical scenarios with multiple data sources.
